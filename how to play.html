How To Play Chess

To play Chess you first need to learn what the pieces are, how to use them and where to put them.

The chess pieces are: The pawn, bishop, knight, rook, queen & king

The Pawn: There are a total of 16 pawns on the board all set up next to each other on the 2nd and 7nd ranks. Pawns can only move one square forward except on there first move when they can move two squares forward. Pawns can only capture things diagonally except when an en passent is available(You will learn about that later).

The Bishop: There is a total of 4 bishops on the board all on the 3rd square of each end on the 1st and 8th ranks. Bishops move and capture diagonally.

The Knight: There are a total of 4 knight on the board all on the 2nd square of each end on the 1st and 8th ranks. Knights move and capture in L shapes.

The Rook: There are a total of 4 rooks on the board all on the 1st square of each end on the 1st and 8th ranks. Rooks move and capture horizontally and vertically.

The Queen: There are a total of 2 queens on the board all on the square next to the bishop that is next to the color of the queen. Queens move and capture diagonally, horizontally and vertically.

The King: There are a total of two kings on the board all next to the queen and the bishop. Kings can move and captur any direction one square except when it castles(You will learn about that later). WARNING If the king is trapped and cannot move or go anywhere and you can not capture the piece attacking the king, YOU LOSE THE GAME!
